## The scope

Digital Best Start in Life supports the wider vision for improving children’s health by making it easier for families to access information and use digital services.

As part of this, the Blood Spot Notifications pilot focuses on improving how parents receive newborn screening results. It aims to create a more accessible, timely and user-friendly experience, while reducing reliance on existing manual and paper-based processes.

---

## The aims

### For parents

- Improve the experience of receiving screening results
- Make results easier to access
- Reduce delays and anxiety while waiting for results
- Remove reliance on postal letters

### For the NHS

- Reduce pressure on existing systems
- Reduce costs associated with printing and sending letters
- Reduce administrative workload linked to processing and managing letters

---

## The problem

### Receiving and understanding the letter

From research and stakeholder feedback, we found that:

- Letters can be lost or overlooked
- Content is not always easy to understand, particularly for families with different language needs
- Health visitors often need to explain or translate results

---

## The proposal

During Alpha, we recommend making screening results available directly in the NHS App, within the child’s profile.

Parents would receive a simple notification when a “not suspected” result is ready to view.

This approach:

- Supports the Digital Red Book ambition to digitise child health information
- Provides a more consistent and joined-up experience for families
- Allows delivery to progress without dependency on more complex national proxy services
- Enables value to be delivered sooner

---

## The journey

We carried out research with 7 parents of infants under 12 months old to understand how screening results are currently communicated and how this could be improved.

We explored three formats:

- The existing printed letter
- A digital version of the letter (message format)
- A results page within the child’s record in the NHS App

We also reviewed the national template for the “not suspected” letter used by Child Health Information Services (CHIS). This informed our initial digital prototypes, including:

- Revised letter content for digital use
- A structured results page within the NHS App

These prototypes were tested side by side in research sessions. This allowed us to:

- Compare how parents understood each format
- Identify differences in preferences and behaviours
- Understand what information parents look for first

We used these insights to iterate the digital designs and make evidence-based recommendations for improving the existing letter. Content and layouts will continue to be tested in future rounds.

---

## The results

Parents consistently found the results page design easier to understand than both the existing letter and the digital letter format.

The main reason for this was clarity and prioritisation of information. The results page:

- Clearly states the outcome (“no further action needed”) at the top
- Highlights the most important information first
- Reduces the need to read or interpret long blocks of text or medical terminology

Participants told us they would prefer to receive results in this format alone, rather than alongside a letter or message.
