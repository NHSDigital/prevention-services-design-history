The team successfully completed a significant programme milestone last week, transferring the DES Helpdesk function from Health Information Consulting (HIC) a third-party supplier to the National Service Desk (NSD).

This forms part of a wider programme that will see NHS teams progressively assume responsibility for additional supplier-managed services and processes. The approach is designed to strengthen internal expertise, improve service resilience, and support the long-term sustainability of national services.

A key objective of the transition has been to build knowledge and capability within NHS teams. Through onboarding the DES Helpdesk function, National Service Desk colleagues have developed expertise across new products and services, increasing operational resilience while reducing reliance on external suppliers.

The transition has also created additional capacity within supplier organisations, including HIC, enabling them to focus on ongoing cohorting activities and preparations for future service transfers.


### Key outcomes delivered

The transition has enabled:

-	Successful migration of the DES helpdesk function to the National Service Desk
-	Development of internal expertise across new products and service areas
-	Increased capability within NHS-managed support services
-	Reduced dependency on third-party suppliers for service delivery
-	Greater supplier capacity to support wider programme activity
-   A strong foundation for future service transfers


## Supporting future change

The successful delivery of this first transition demonstrates the value of the phased approach being adopted across the programme.

Lessons learned from the DES Helpdesk migration will inform future service transfers, helping to ensure that knowledge transfer, operational readiness, and service continuity remain central to delivery planning.

The transition also marks an important step in the continued evolution of the National Service Desk. Over the next year, additional service areas and query types will progressively move into NSD, further expanding its role as a central support function. This evolution will continue alongside the rollout of the new cohorting solution, helping to create a more integrated, sustainable, and resilient support model for national services.

As cohorting work progresses, the programme will build on this experience to bring further supplier-managed services into NHS operations. This will strengthen long-term sustainability, enhance organisational capability, and support the delivery of national service objectives.
