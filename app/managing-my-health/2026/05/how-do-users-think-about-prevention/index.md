When we asked 11 people to sort NHS prevention services into groups, they didn't see prevention as one thing – they grouped services by who initiates the service and the user's mindset. Roughly speaking:

- things the NHS contacts you about
- things you do when you have a problem
- things you explore when you're curious

This early finding is shaping how we think about prevention in the NHS App.

## Background

Managing My Health is a new team in the Digital Prevention Services Portfolio (DPSP) (see our intro post on [what Managing My Health is](/managing-my-health/2026/04/what-is-managing-my-health/)). As we shape candidates for our first discovery, we wanted to understand:

- what existing research already tells us about people's needs from prevention services
- how people group and think about different services – vaccines, screening, health checks, behaviour change tools
- where people expect to find these in the NHS App

## What we did

### Desk research

Before running interviews, we reviewed existing research from across NHS England to understand what was already known. This covered user research from teams working on vaccines, screening, test results, and the NHS App more broadly.

The desk research gave us a foundation of understanding and shaped our discussion guide. Key findings are summarised below.

### Generative interviews

We ran 11 remote, 60‑minute 1:1 interviews. Participants were recruited across 2 age cohorts relevant to prevention eligibility – adults aged 25 to 45 (eligible for cervical screening) and adults aged 65 to 74 (eligible for vaccines and other screening). We recruited for a range of tech confidence levels, health conditions, caring responsibilities, and ethnicities across UK locations.

Interviews included a structured discussion of the NHS App and prevention, prototype testing of early concepts, and a card sorting exercise to explore mental models for prevention services.

## What we found

### From desk research – people want to know what's due and see their history

Existing research across DPSP points to clear user needs for prevention services in the app:

- **Personalised eligibility** – users want to know which vaccines and screenings are due, based on their age and individual circumstances
- **Results and history** – users want access to past results as a reference for ongoing medical care, to check when they last had a screening or vaccine, or simply as a source of reassurance
- **Booking support** – users want to arrange or reschedule appointments based on their availability

### From interviews – 3 service groups

Across our 11 interviews, the most consistent finding came from a card sort. We gave participants cards for prevention services, with descriptions – including vaccines, screening, NHS Health Check, Weight Management, Talking Therapies, stop smoking support, and the AI Health Coach. We asked participants to sort the cards into groups that made sense to them.

Clear patterns appeared across participants. People grouped services along similar lines, often using similar language to describe what they were doing. With 11 participants this is not conclusive, but it gives us a direction.

#### NHS‑initiated services

Vaccines and screening were consistently grouped together as things the NHS contacts you about and expects you to do. Users described these as routine, expected, and something you respond to rather than seek out. Some also included the NHS Health Check in this category.

#### Self-help services

Weight management, stop smoking, alcohol reduction, and Talking Therapies grouped together. Participants saw these as issue‑specific things you go to when you have a particular problem to fix. Multiple participants used the phrase "self‑help" without being prompted.

#### Lifestyle tools

The NHS Health Check, heart health check, AI Health Coach, and Healthy Choices Quiz grouped together. These were seen as things you explore when curious or when you have time to engage with your health – optional rather than obligatory.

One participant described the distinction as they saw it:

> Maybe you don't look for quit smoking advice where you book your shingles vaccine, just in terms of the mentality with which you're clicking on it.

## What's next

These early findings suggest prevention in the app might be organised around how users think – not how the NHS is structured. We'll use this as we shape the long‑term direction of prevention services in the app, and decide on our first discovery.
