The NHS App is changing from a digital front door to a lifelong companion. The app will meet people where they are, on demand. Managing my health shapes and supports Digital Prevention Services' role in that future.

We sit between prevention services and the app so it is easy for people to stay healthy – for themselves and the people they care for. To do this, we:
* work with the app team as it shifts to federated model
* help decide when to join services to better meet user needs
* support shared patterns for a more consistent user experience
* start small and learn as we go

## Hypothesis

The NHS App can help more people get the prevention support that is right for them, sooner – by giving them simpler, more relevant choices and reducing the friction between the steps.

We think this works in 2 ways – by reaching people who don't engage with prevention services today, and by offering a clearer route to those who do.

## Outcomes

1. The app can reach people who currently face the biggest barriers to prevention, not just those already engaged
2. People can sustain the changes they make, with support that adapts as their needs change.
3. More prevention services are available in the NHS App, with a consistent experience across them. Teams can deliver faster, without duplicating effort.

## Framing prevention in the app

Prevention is broad. Instead of attempting to approach it as one thing, we've framed it as 4 user experiences – what people need from prevention at different points in their lives:
1. I know what I’ve had, what I’ve missed, and what’s next
2. I get joined‑up care from screening to treatment
3. I find out about my risks and act on them
4. I manage my conditions and stop them getting worse

## Riskiest assumptions

Before proposing solutions, we named the assumptions that mattered most. If any of these turned out to be wrong, we'd need to rethink how this work could be done.

### User behaviour

* people will engage with prevention through the app more than through current channels
* people will follow through from the app to real-world action

### Data

* the data we need exists, is accurate, and can be accessed safely
* the data we have today is good enough to make a meaningful difference now

### Clinical and governance

* prevention content can sit safely alongside clinical services, without users mistaking one for the other
* programmes and the app team will support this way of working

### Design

* we can build safely for when targeting fails or data is missing
* patterns built for one prevention service can be reused across others

## What’s next

We're researching how people currently understand prevention – the language they use, the routes they take, the support they expect. From that, we'll choose a first discovery to test the riskiest assumptions in this post.
